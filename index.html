Ratan Naval Tata

A Legacy of Innovation

  • 1937:Ratan Naval Tata is born on December 28th in Mumbai, India.
  • 1962:Graduates from Cornell University with a degree in architecture.
  • 1971:Joins the Tata Group as an executive trainee.
  • 1991:Becomes the chairman of the Tata Group, succeeding his uncle, J.R.D. Tata.
  • 1998:Launches the Tata Indica, India's first indigenously developed passenger car.
  • 2008:Successfully acquires Jaguar Land Rover from Ford Motor Company.
  • 2009:Introduces the Tata Nano, the world's cheapest car, revolutionizing the Indian automobile market.
  • 2012: Steps down as chairman of the Tata Group but remains an active member of the board.
  • 2015: Receives the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award, for his contributions to industry and philanthropy.
  • 2019: Celebrates his 82nd birthday, continuing to inspire and influence countless individuals.
  • 2024: Passes away peacefully on October 9th at the age of 86.
